Make an appeal to the tax tribunal

Use this service to challenge decisions by HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC).

The tribunal is independent of government and will listen to both sides of the argument before making a decision.

What it costs

You may have to pay tax tribunal fees of up to £200 to start an appeal, and further fees depending on your case. You’ll get these back if you’re successful.

You may want to resolve your matter directly with HMRC at no cost using its review and resolution services.

If you’re on a low income or receiving certain benefits, you may be able to get help with fees to make an appeal.

Start now

Before you start

  • You’ll need your decision letter from HMRC
  • You’ll need a debit or credit card to pay your appeal fee
  • You can also use this service to appeal certain decisions made by the UK Border Force (UKBF) and National Crime Agency (NCA)

Other ways to apply

  • You can apply by paper form
  • Send your application
    • by email to taxappeals@hmcts.gsi.gov.uk
    • by post to First-tier Tribunal (Tax Chamber), PO Box 16972, Birmingham, B16 6TZ
