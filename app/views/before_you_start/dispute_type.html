What is your dispute about?

Your dispute will be stated on either the original notice letter from HMRC, or the review conclusion letter if your case was reviewed.

You can't combine separate disputes on separate letters into one appeal.

Help with types of dispute

The type of HMRC decision will be stated on the original notice letter you received from HMRC, or the review conclusion letter if you had a review with HMRC.

The last official decision letter you received from HMRC provides important details for your case. The type of tax, the amount of tax, or any penalty that you are being asked to pay will be outlined in this letter. The details in your letter essentially form the basis of your appeal to the independent tribunal.

You can only submit an appeal to an independent tribunal based on the contents of a single decision or review letter.

You can't combine separate disputes on separate letters into one appeal.
