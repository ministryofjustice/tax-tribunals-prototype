1. Find out the cost of your appeal

You will need to pay an initial fee of £20, £50 or £200 unless you are on a low income or certain benefits.

You will also have to pay a further fee if your case is more complex and needs a hearing in person with a judge.

The judge has the power to decide whether your fees are refunded by the unsuccessful party.

Appeal against a tax decision

You will need to enter your appeal details, give reasons if you want to appeal late, and upload the correct documents.

What you will need

  • a scan or photo of the original notice letter, or review conclusion letter, so it's ready to upload in a digital format
  • reasons if you're appealing late so the judge can decide whether to accept your appeal after the deadline
  • written explanation for your appeal to copy and paste or attach as a document
  • signed approval (PDF, 58Kb) if you want someone to act on your behalf and receive letters from the tax tribunal or attend a hearing

Warning You can't save details so get everything ready before you start
