Appeal to a tax tribunal

The Ministry of Justice (MOJ) runs the tax tribunal service. Its powers are completely independent of government.

A judge listens to both sides in the dispute before making an impartial decision on your case. For more information see the guide to tax tribunals.

How to prepare your appeal

  • scan your letters from HMRC - you will need to upload the original notice letter or review conclusion letter from HMRC
  • write your grounds of appeal - say why you think any decisions are wrong so the tribunal can hear things from your side
  • check if you can get help with fees - if you're on certain benefits or a low income you may get money off your tribunal fees

You can't save details as you go. Please get everything ready before you start.

Other ways to submit an appeal

  • You can submit an appeal or application by paper form
  • Send your form by post to First-tier Tribunal (Tax Chamber), PO Box 16972, Birmingham, B16 6TZ
