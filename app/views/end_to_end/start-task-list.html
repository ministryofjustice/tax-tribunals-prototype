Appeal to a tax tribunal

The tax tribunal is run by the Ministry of Justice. Its decision-making authority is completely independent of government.

A judge will listen to both sides in the dispute before making an impartial ruling on your case. For more information see the guide to tax tribunals.

How to prepare your appeal

  • scan letters from HMRC - you will need to upload the original notice letter or review conclusion letter from HMRC
  • write your grounds of appeal - the tribunal will want to hear things from your side and why you think the decision is wrong
  • check if you get help with fees - if you're on certain benefits or a low income you may get money off your fees

You can't save details as you go. Please get everything ready before you start.

Other ways to submit an appeal

  • You can submit an appeal or application by paper form
  • Send your form by post to First-tier Tribunal (Tax Chamber), PO Box 16972, Birmingham, B16 6TZ
