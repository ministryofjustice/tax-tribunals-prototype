Appeal to the tax tribunal

The Ministry of Justice (MOJ) runs the tax tribunal service. Every case is decided by an independent judge.

If you can't come to an agreement with HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC), a judge will listen to both sides in the dispute before making an impartial decision on your case.

For more information see the guide to tax tribunals.

What you will need before you start

  • digital copies of the decision letter(s) from HMRC - scan or take a photo of the original notice letter (or review conclusion letter if you had a review)
  • reasons why you're appealing - write out your grounds of appeal so they are ready to copy and paste or upload as a document
  • a way to pay your tribunal fee - you will need to pay an initial fee unless you're on certain benefits or a low income and can get help with fees
  • approval to act for someone else - attach authorisation from the taxpayer if you want to receive correspondence or act as their representative

Warning You can't save details as you go. Please get everything ready before you start.

Other ways to submit an appeal

  • You can use paper forms to submit an appeal (T240) or close an enquiry (T245)
  • Send your form by post to First-tier Tribunal (Tax Chamber), PO Box 16972, Birmingham, B16 6TZ
