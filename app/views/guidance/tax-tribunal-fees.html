3. Tax tribunal fees

You will need to pay an initial fee of £20, £50 or £200 to submit an appeal or application to the tribunal unless you're eligible for help with fees.

Your initial fee contributes towards the cost of processing your case. You may also have to pay a further fee to cover costs if your case needs further documents, correspondence and a hearing in person with a judge.

How to pay or get help with fees

You will have to pay your initial fee to HM Courts and Tribunals Service (HMCTS) when you submit details for your case.

Check before you start your appeal whether you can get help paying your fees if you’re on a low income or receiving certain benefits.

Warning You will need to submit the correct documents and pay an initial fee for your appeal to proceed.

You can pay tax tribunal fees by:

  • credit or debit card, if you are submitting an appeal online
  • authorised account, if you have an account set-up with HMCTS
  • cheque, if you are submitting your appeal by post
  • help with fees reference number, if you have successfully applied for help with fees

Claiming a refund

The judge is independent of government and has the power to decide whether your fees are refunded. The tax tribunal itself does not have the power to enforce or award costs.
