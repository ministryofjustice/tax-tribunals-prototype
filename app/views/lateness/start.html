2. Check you meet tribunal deadlines

You usually have up to 30 days to appeal, unless your case is under review or you have reasons for being late.

You should appeal within the time stated on your last letter from HMRC.

If you accepted or asked for a review, you must allow time for it to end. Your 30 days to appeal begins once you get a review conclusion letter or 45 days passes without receiving one.

You can still appeal late if you have good reasons, although your case risks being rejected if the judge does not accept them.

What you will need

  • date you received your last letter from HMRC
  • reasons why you're outside the time limit if you want to submit an appeal late
