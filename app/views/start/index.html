Appeal to the tax tribunal

The Ministry of Justice (MOJ) runs the tax tribunal service. Every case is decided by an independent judge.

Try and settle your dispute directly first. If you can't, a judge will listen to both sides before making an impartial decision on your case. For more information see the guide to tax tribunals.

What you will need before you start

  • digital copies of the decision letter(s) - scan or take a photo of the original notice or decision letter (or review conclusion letter if you had a review)
  • reasons why you're appealing - write out your grounds of appeal so they are ready to copy and paste or upload as a document
  • a way to pay your initial tribunal fee - you can pay by debit/credit online or you may get help with fees if you're on a low income or certain benefits
  • approval to act for someone else - you will need authorisation from the taxpayer (PDF, 58Kb) to receive correspondence or act as their representative

Warning You can't save details as you go. Please get everything ready before you start.

Other ways to submit an appeal

  • You can use paper forms to submit an appeal (T240) or close an enquiry (T245)
  • Send your form by post to First-tier Tribunal (Tax Chamber), PO Box 16972, Birmingham, B16 6TZ
