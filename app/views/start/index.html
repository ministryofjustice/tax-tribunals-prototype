Appeal to the tax tribunal

The Ministry of Justice (MOJ) runs the tax tribunal service.

Try and settle your dispute directly first. If you can't, a judge will listen to both sides and make an impartial decision on your case.

For more information see the guide to tax tribunals.

What to prepare

  • a scan or photo of the original notice letter or review conclusion letter so it's ready to upload in a digital format
  • written reasons for your appeal to copy and paste or attach as a document
  • a way to pay your appeal fee such as a debit or credit card, unless you are on a low income or certain benefits
  • signed approval (PDF, 58Kb) if you want someone to receive correspondence or act on your behalf at a hearing

Warning You can't save details so get everything ready before you start

Other ways to submit an appeal

  • You can use paper forms to submit an appeal (T240) or close an enquiry (T245)
  • Send your form by post to First-tier Tribunal (Tax Chamber), PO Box 16972, Birmingham, B16 6TZ
